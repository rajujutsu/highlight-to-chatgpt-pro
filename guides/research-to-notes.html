Research → Notes (Copy/Paste Killer)

Your goal isn’t “a summary.” Your goal is a note you can reuse. This template turns highlights into structured notes that drop cleanly into Notion or Docs.

Best for

Articles, documentation, Reddit threads, product pages — anything you want to capture as reusable notes.

Copy-ready template (Notion/Docs note)

ROLE: You are a research assistant. TASK: Turn the highlighted text into a reusable note. CONSTRAINTS: - Accurate; no invented facts. - Plain language. - If the highlight contains claims, label them as "claims" (not facts) unless proven. OUTPUT FORMAT: Title: (short) Summary: (2–3 sentences) Key points: (4–7 bullets) Important terms: (define any jargon) Quotes worth saving: (1–3 short quotes max) Open questions: (what’s missing / what to look up next) HIGHLIGHT: {HIGHLIGHT}

Upgrade: add your purpose

Notes get 10x better when you tell it why you’re saving this.

Add this line: PURPOSE: I’m saving this because: {your reason}. Examples: - "I’m writing a blog post." - "I’m comparing tools." - "I’m studying for an exam."

Upgrade: extract “useful fields”

Perfect for product pages or how-tos.

Add this section to the OUTPUT FORMAT: Useful fields: - Who is this for? - What problem does it solve? - Steps/process (if any) - Pros/cons (if any) - Risks/limits (if any)

Pro template: research → decision

Turn reading into a decision you can act on.

ROLE: You are a research analyst. TASK: Convert the highlight into a decision-ready brief. CONSTRAINTS: - Separate facts vs claims. - Include tradeoffs. OUTPUT FORMAT: 1) TL;DR decision: (one sentence recommendation) 2) Evidence: (bullets; facts only) 3) Claims: (bullets; label as claims) 4) Tradeoffs: (pros/cons) 5) Risks: (what could go wrong) 6) Next step: (one action) HIGHLIGHT: {HIGHLIGHT}
